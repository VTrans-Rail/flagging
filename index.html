Flagging Request

To occupy the railroad right-of-way, you must request flagging protection from the railroad.

Make Request

The Process

  1. Submit a request
  2. VTrans reviews request
  3. VRS reviews request
  4. Railroad flagger contacts you

What is Flagging

To protect people and equipment working in the railroad right-of-way, a railroad "flagger" accompanies you during your work window. They are in direct contact with the train dispatchers, and have a "track warrant" which gives them control of the track and prohibits a train from entering the space.

Why is it Required?

The State of Vermont, specifically the Agency of Transportation, owns many miles of active railroad. They lease the operation of the railroad to a private company - Vermont Rail Systems - who oversee the movement of goods over these rail lines.

As the property owner, VTrans needs to oversee all occupations of State lands to ensure:

  1. The railroad can continue to operate safely
  2. The State collects the fair-market fees for the occupations

Advance Notice

The railroad has requested a minimum of ten (10) days advanced notice for flagging protection. Requests outside of this window will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, and will likely require you to reschedule you request.

Allowances

Only organizations with existing agreements to occupy the railroad right of way will be granted flagging protection. If you need to setup or ammend an agreement, please contact property management.

Minimum Times

You must book at least 4 hours of time.

Fees

The railroad will bill you at $85/hr.
